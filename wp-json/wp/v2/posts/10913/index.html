Four hundred and fifty bawalis of Bagerhat make a living by cutting and selling the leaves of round trees of Sundarbans. However, Golpata has to be collected as per the time fixed by the forest department. Fishermen will start collecting golpata from the morning of January 28 (Sunday) this year. Will continue till March 31. On Sunday morning, the Bawalis will go to the Sundarbans with the permission (pass-permit) from the forest department. In the meantime, they have completed all kinds of preparations to collect golpata.<\/p>\n

Bawali Mozammel Howladar, a resident of South Char of Mongla, who has been making a living by extracting gol leaves for 13 years, said, “We have made boats, buses, ropes and sharp knives to go to cut gol leaves. I will go to Sundarbans with permission from the forest department.<\/p>\n

Bawali Siddiqur Rahman of Matherpar area of \u200b\u200bSharankhola said that every year the boat needs to be repaired before the collection of golpata. Golpata does not work like before. Good prices are not available. As the price of everything has increased, it is difficult to run the family. Even so, to sustain the profession, I go to cut golpata every year. Besides, the boats are made in a special way for extracting Golpata. Due to which the boat has to be abandoned after the season of golpata extraction is over. He said that because of leaving, extra money is spent for repairs every year.<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
